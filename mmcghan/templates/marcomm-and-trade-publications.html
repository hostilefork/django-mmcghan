Straight up, I've always cringed at the idea of being a spin doctor, so you're not going to find any "advertorial" content here that makes extravagant claims about products that can make you hot, rich, or thin, and you won't find anything hawking the wares of companies with questionable business practices. I've specialized in profiles of hardworking business owners either flying solo or in a partnership, oftentimes with quirky, unique products. One of the more fun pieces I've written was the one on Etsy artisans in the Las Vegas area. For Exhibit City News, I profiled a number of women in the male-dominated trade show industry.

As a contributing editor to Great Lakes Poker and Gaming, I learned about the gaming industry through profiling its insiders, though the closest I've ever come to gaming(despite living in Las Vegas for years) is playing slots a couple of times while waiting for movies to begin. (Yes, like everything else in Las Vegas, movie theaters are inside casinos). Understanding how the mind of an ace poker player works is fascinating -- the combination of logic and intuition you find among the big winners is pretty rare.

During the worst part of the recession, before I left Las Vegas, I was lucky enough to find work at the U.S. Census Bureau for 18 months. About a third of that time was spent as a Census recruiter, testing prospective enumerators. I was able to parlay this into a short-lived position as a technical recruiter after moving to Austin. It seemed like it might be a bridge to technical writing; it definitely garnered me a bit of freelance, writing a few articles for business executives about how to choose a technical recruiter. (Ultimately, technical recruiting was not a great fit for a relatively introverted person such as myself, and I returned to a politically oriented day job as election season 2012 heated up, but that's another story).
