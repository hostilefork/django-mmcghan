Journalism

Environmental News

I wrote for Las Vegas CityLife, an alternative weekly, for several years, first as a staffer and later as a freelancer. I met the CityLife editorial team when I won the magazine's fiction and poetry contest.

I started as a part-time newsroom assistant while still in graduate school and teaching classes as a TA. After my last semester of coursework, they offered me a full-time job as calendar editor and staff writer. This position involved all phases of newsroom production. I maintained a lengthy events calendar for the metropolitan Las Vegas area, wrote stories, and copyedited the work of other writers. I also contributed ideas to the magazine's redesign. At my suggestion, the calendar was pared down considerably, with important events highlighted.

Las Vegas, being a heavily populated desert city, dealt with many sustainability issues. I've chosen three stories as examples of how commerce, science, and politics can clash in our increasingly turbulent era. With its years-long drought, excess-driven economy, and early vulnerability to the recession, Las Vegas is a harbinger of climate change and economic boom-bust cycles for the rest of the country. Community Issues

I'm drawn to seek out stories that show the vast amount of improvement needed in our society's institutions — whether schools, nursing homes, or shelters. It's clear that the warehousing of persons often leads to neglect and abuse, and that people need the economic and legal freedom to engage alternatives. Authenticity is a major concern regarding the information we receive. False information, including various forms of spam, is increasing exponentially due to the Internet. One of the most important tasks we face as modern consumers of information is learning to filter this "noise" out and recognize factual information.
