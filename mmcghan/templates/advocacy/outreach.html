My Non-Profit Outreach Project

Forays I've made into technology have shown me that seemingly minor decisions can drastically affect the present and future success of a website. So I'm on a new mission:

Help nonprofits develop a better Web presence.

I've been involved with nonprofits for years, doing both fundraising and advocacy. I'm proactively scouting out good causes, and offering my insights about the following issues (and/or assisting in fixing them):

  • The organization does not own its domain name
  • The group cannot be found on a Web search of its name and/or services
  • Web writing with grammar, punctuation, and spelling errors
  • Wordiness with no keywords or phrases
  • No clear brand that sets the organization apart
  • Pages that are hard to navigate
  • Awkward URLs (too long or ending in things like index.php)
  • Graphics that look like clip art, not unique
  • A logo that does not convey information
  • Slow-loading pages
  • Poor color, typography, and layout choices
  • Lack of transparency, no sense of being a legitimate organization
  • No website, relying solely on social media for their Internet presence

Each of these weaknesses can lead to greater problems, and most sites that have one of these issues have several. Ultimately, having a bad website--or none--will hurt an organization's membership, donations, and effectiveness.

The Internet is complex, and it's easy to be unaware of consequences. As I was making meredithmcghan.com, I blogged on a lot of the topics I was learning about. Recently I described the maze of issues involved in just putting your recipes online...and that is nothing compared to the set of rules that come into play when managing something far more important like an organization's entire online identity!

I'm eager to volunteer what I know by pointing out what I can, and will do work at a discounted rate for 501(c)(3) organizations. If anything comes up that requires more programming or graphic design talents than my modest present abilities, I can call upon my wizard friends who share my desire to help good causes to coordinate getting it done.

There's my plan. But if I don't contact you, feel free to Contact Me!
