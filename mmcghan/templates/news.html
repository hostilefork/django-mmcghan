Investigative Journalism & News

Environmental News

I wrote for Las Vegas CityLife, an alternative weekly, for several years, first as a staffer and later as a freelancer. Las Vegas, being a heavily populated desert city, dealt with many sustainability issues. I've chosen four stories -- on water rights, energy usage, public transportation and growth — as examples of how commerce, science, and politics can clash in our increasingly turbulent era. With its years-long drought, excess-driven economy, and early vulnerability to the recession, Las Vegas is a harbinger of climate change and economic boom-bust cycles for the rest of the country.

Community Issues

I'm drawn to seek out stories that show the vast amount of improvement needed in our society's institutions — whether schools, nursing homes or shelters. It's clear that the warehousing of persons can lead to various kinds of neglect and abuse, and that people need the economic and legal freedom to engage alternatives.

Perhaps idealistically, I grew up thinking of "the news" as an abstract institution, one whose purpose was to keep the others in check. That's why I included the article about a video news release featuring a public relations spokesperson that one of the Las Vegas TV stations aired as real news. Authenticity is a major concern regarding the information we receive. False information, including various forms of spam, is increasing exponentially due to the Internet. One of the most important tasks we face as modern consumers of information is learning to filter this "noise" out and recognize factual information.
