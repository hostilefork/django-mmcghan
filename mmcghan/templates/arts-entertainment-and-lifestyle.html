Arts, Entertainment, & Lifestyle

I started my career as an arts and entertainment writer, and I've included a few of the most fun reviews I ever had the pleasure to write in those early days. If I have to pick an all-time early favorite, though, it'd have to be the story on a Flash animation series which was one of the earliest on the Internet: "Radiskull and Devil Doll." I even had a Candy Angel t-shirt, which I wore until the decal cracked and faded into oblivion:

However, I always identified more with Radiskull, since without frequent cups of coffee (boiling hot!) he is prone to narcolepsy, and without caffeine none of this — or anything of mine — would have been written. I also hate snow

Living and writing in Las Vegas for several years, I had many opportunities to see some pretty awesome shows. Of all the shows on the Strip, the Blue Man Group and the offerings of Cirque du Soleil, such as Mystere, were my favorites.

As a contributing editor for two lifestyle startup magazines, Business Executive Lifestyle and the suburban versions of Las Vegas' What's On!, I wrote copy, hired freelancers and assigned pieces to them, and copyedited other writers' work. I helped plan the editorial calendar, and even picked up a bit of graphic design knowledge along the way.
